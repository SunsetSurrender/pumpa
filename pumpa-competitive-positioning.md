# Pumpa — Competitive positioning

Grounded in an actual scan of the field (July 2026). Purpose: sharpen home-page copy,
Tips article angles, and feature priorities against real competitors. The headline finding:
**almost every individual feature Pumpa is building already exists somewhere.** The edge is
the *combination + the medium (web) + the design + a stated privacy value* — not any single
feature. Positioning must reflect that.

---

## The competitive landscape (four tiers)

**1. Bare web calculators** — travelcost.eu, fleetnews.co.uk, RAC, ndtv.
Stateless (calculate + forget), often ad-heavy or dated. travelcost.eu is the closest to
Pumpa's *strategy* (calculator + EV page + content on fuel/eco/tolls/tax) and ranks in the
EU — so the "calculator + articles" SEO lane is NOT empty. But they're visually poor and
don't log anything. **Beatable on: design, logging, UX.**
Worth stealing: Fleet News auto-fills consumption from a car make/model dropdown — removes
the "I don't know my L/100km" friction. (Pumpa's cheaper version: the consumption-range input.)

**2. Simple calculator apps** — "Fuel Calculator", "Fuelito", "Fuel Calculators – Trip Cost".
More capable than expected: multi-unit, 70+ languages, offline, light/dark, and some already
ship multi-leg trip planning, cost-splitting, price comparison, and **explicit EV-vs-gas
comparison**. So EV comparison + trip planning is already commoditised in app stores.
**Not a differentiator on its own — but they're apps, not websites.**

**3. Full fuel trackers — the real benchmark: FUELIO.**
Study this one. It already does: fill-up logging, full-tank consumption algorithm, monthly
cost charts, CSV export, gas-price crowdsourcing + nearest stations on a map, cloud backup,
multi-vehicle. **This is essentially Pumpa's entire roadmap, already shipped, on Android.**
Pumpa does not beat Fuelio on features. Pumpa beats it (potentially) on: being a website
(no install), design/polish, multi-market unit handling, and privacy-as-a-headline (Fuelio
buries local storage; offers cloud sync).

**4. Road-trip apps with live prices + maps** — Roadtrip, GasBuddy.
Origin/destination routing, exact car specs, cost-splitting, nearby cheapest stations,
150k+ stations with live prices (GasBuddy, US). This is who the map feature competes with.
They're app-first and (GasBuddy) US-centric. **The EU web lane for "cheapest fuel near me"
is less saturated — that's the map's opening.**

---

## Where Pumpa actually wins (lead with these)

1. **It's a website, not an app.** No install, works on any device, and — critically —
   *ranks on Google*. The trackers (Fuelio, Roadtrip, GasBuddy) are app-store downloads;
   they've largely ceded web search. travelcost owns the web lane but is ugly. This is the
   single biggest structural advantage. "No app to install" is a headline, not a footnote.

2. **Design & polish.** Every tier 1–3 competitor is utilitarian or cluttered. In a category
   this aesthetically poor, looking genuinely premium (the hero, the LCD readout, the bands)
   is a real moat. This is Pumpa's most visible edge on first contact.

3. **Privacy / local-first as a STATED VALUE.** Competitors bury "stored locally" or push
   cloud accounts. Pumpa makes "your data never leaves your device, no account, nothing
   uploaded" a headline. Genuine differentiator in a category full of sign-ups.

4. **Multi-market done properly.** A *tracker* (not just a calculator) that works across
   metric/US/UK + any currency + (eventually) EU fuel-price maps. Most trackers are
   single-country for prices; most unit-flexible tools are stateless calculators. That
   intersection is thin.

5. **The combination in one clean place.** Calculator + petrol/EV + trip planning + logging +
   reports + (coming) maps — each exists elsewhere, but rarely all together, on the web,
   looking this good, without an account.

---

## Where Pumpa does NOT win (don't claim these)

- **Not on raw feature count** — Fuelio and the trip apps match or exceed the feature list.
- **Not on live-price coverage** — GasBuddy's 150k+ US stations, established price crowdsourcing.
- **Not on auto-tracking** — GPS trip logging, car-model spec databases: competitors have
  these; Pumpa is manual entry (which privacy-first framing can turn into a virtue, but
  don't pretend it's more automated than it is).

---

## Copy & content implications

- **Home page should lead with "tracker, not calculator" + "web, no install" + "private".**
  Current copy leans on "honest numbers" and "your data stays yours" — good, keep the
  privacy line, but ADD the "remembers your driving / no app needed" angle none of the copy
  currently states. The competitive gap is *memory + medium*, so say it.
- **Tips articles**: the "EV vs petrol running costs" and "real consumption vs manufacturer
  figure" angles are strong because they map to Pumpa's actual differentiated features
  (both-calculator comparison; fill-up-based real consumption). Keep those central.
- **Don't over-claim.** These competitors are real and some are good. Pumpa wins on medium +
  polish + privacy + coherence, not on out-featuring a mature app like Fuelio. Honest
  positioning ages better than "the best fuel app ever."

## One idea worth stealing (backlog)
Fleet News / Roadtrip auto-fill consumption from car make/model. Removes the biggest
first-use friction ("I don't know my consumption"). Full make/model DB is real data work;
the cheaper 80% is Pumpa's own **consumption-range input** (enter low/typical/high → cost
range). Prioritise the range input; treat a model database as a later, data-heavy maybe.
