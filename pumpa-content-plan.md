# Pumpa — Tips launch content plan

Six articles for launch. Rationale: enough that the Tips section looks alive and
authoritative (not 2-3 thin stubs), few enough that you're not guessing at scale before
search data tells you what works. Quality per article beats count — aim ~1,000–1,500 words
each, genuinely answering the query. Each must be written in EN, then carefully translated
to IT/ES (6 × 3 = 18 pieces — real work; the EN set is the priority, translations follow).

Ordering roughly by search value + how well each pulls a distinct audience.

---

## 1. MPG vs L/100km vs km/L — how to actually compare fuel economy
**Intent:** "mpg to l/100km", "what is l/100km", "convert fuel consumption" — high-volume,
evergreen, and it's the exact confusion Pumpa's unit system solves. Natural tool tie-in.
**Angle:** Plain explainer of why the numbers feel backwards (MPG higher = better, L/100km
lower = better — the inverse relationship), how to convert, and why "good" economy differs
by country. Ends: "Pumpa converts this for you automatically."
**Outline:** What each unit means → why higher/lower flip → the conversion (worked example)
→ what counts as good in metric vs US vs UK → common mistakes → try it in the calculator.

## 2. EV vs petrol: what a commute actually costs to run
**Intent:** "ev vs petrol running cost", "is an electric car cheaper", "cost per mile EV" —
growing, high-intent, and it ties BOTH your calculators together. This is the article that
serves your highest-upside audience.
**Angle:** Honest, neutral cost comparison — cost per 100km/mile for a typical petrol car vs
EV, how home vs public charging changes it, how fuel/electricity price swings flip the math.
No greenwashing; purely cost. Ends: "Compare your own car both ways in Pumpa's calculator."
**Outline:** The headline question → petrol cost per distance (worked) → EV cost per distance,
home vs public → what makes EV win or lose (electricity price, charging mix) → the honest
"it depends" → compare your exact numbers in the tool.

## 3. How to actually reduce your fuel consumption (that make a real difference)
**Intent:** "how to save fuel", "reduce fuel consumption", "save money on petrol" — huge
evergreen volume, broad appeal.
**Angle:** Evidence-based, no-nonsense — the changes that genuinely move the needle (tyre
pressure, smooth driving, weight, aircon vs windows, trip planning) vs myths that don't.
Ranks the impact honestly rather than listing 30 tips.
**Outline:** Why small changes add up (with a cost example) → the high-impact ones ranked →
the overrated/myth ones → how to measure if it's working (log it in Pumpa) → track your
real consumption over time.

## 4. Is premium fuel worth it? What you're actually paying for
**Intent:** "is premium petrol worth it", "premium vs regular fuel", "does premium fuel
save money" — strong recurring search, a real question people argue about.
**Angle:** Neutral breakdown — what octane actually does, which cars benefit (few), the
break-even math on the price difference. Data-led, not opinion.
**Outline:** What premium fuel is → what octane does and doesn't do → which cars actually
need/benefit → the cost math (when the premium price is/isn't recouped) → how to test it
for your car by logging consumption both ways.

## 5. Your real fuel economy vs the manufacturer figure — why they never match
**Intent:** "real world mpg", "why is my fuel economy worse than advertised", "wltp vs real"
— relatable frustration, evergreen.
**Angle:** Why official figures (WLTP/EPA) are optimistic, what real-world factors diverge
(cold, short trips, driving style, load), and how to find YOUR true number — which is
exactly what Pumpa's fill-up log + real-consumption report does.
**Outline:** The gap everyone notices → how official figures are measured → why real life
differs → how to measure your true figure (odometer + fill-ups) → Pumpa's real-consumption
report does this automatically.

## 6. The real cost of your commute (and how to see if a detour is worth it)
**Intent:** "cost of commuting calculator", "is my commute worth it", "commute cost per
month" — ties directly to Pumpa's core identity and the trip comparison feature.
**Angle:** How to think about commute cost as a real monthly number, and the "shorter but
tolled vs longer but free" decision — which Pumpa's Trip Plan feature answers directly.
**Outline:** What a commute really costs over a month (worked example) → the hidden trade-offs
(tolls, detours, traffic) → how to compare two routes honestly → seeing the monthly total →
plan it in Pumpa.

## Notes
- **SEO discipline:** each targets a distinct query cluster so they don't compete with each
  other. Titles should match how people actually search (question phrasing works well).
- **Tool tie-in, not tool ad:** each ends with a natural, single pointer to the relevant
  Pumpa feature — helpful, not salesy. The article must stand on its own even if the reader
  never clicks through.
- **After launch:** don't pre-write more. Watch Search Console — write the next batch around
  what actually gets impressions/clicks. Real data beats guessing.
- **Voice:** the warm, honest, plain-spoken Pumpa tone. Genuinely useful, no filler, no
  keyword-stuffing. Localise properly for IT/ES (don't machine-translate and forget — the
  IT article should read as written by an Italian, not translated).
