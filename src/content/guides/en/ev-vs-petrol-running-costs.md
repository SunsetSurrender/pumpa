Set aside the purchase price, depreciation, tax breaks and the climate question
for a moment. Underneath all of it sits a smaller, sharper question: for the
driving you actually do, what does each kilometre cost in energy? That one has
an answer, and you can work it out on the back of a receipt.

running costs.

## Your real number is a mix

Most EV owners charge mostly at home and fast-charge occasionally on longer
trips. Say 80% home, 20% DC:

At 15,000 km:

- Petrol at тВм11.70/100km тАФ **тВм1,755**

## The "it depends" nobody mentions

- **Electricity prices swing more than fuel prices.** A tariff change can move
  your cost per km by 50% overnight, either direction.
- **Winter hits EVs harder.** Cold can add 15тАУ25% to consumption; petrol cars
  suffer too, but less.
- **This is running cost only.** Purchase price, depreciation, maintenance and
  insurance can each outweigh the energy gap. One question answered well beats
  five answered vaguely.
