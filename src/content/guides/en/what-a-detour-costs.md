Everyone has a cheap station they'll drive across town for. Whether that trip
makes sense is not a matter of opinion — it's three numbers.

## Pricing the detour

The detour itself burns fuel. A 12 km round-trip detour in a car using
7 L/100km consumes about **0.84 litres**. At €1.80/L, the detour costs roughly
**€1.51** before you've saved anything.

Now the saving: 4 cents per litre on a 45-litre fill is **€1.80**. Net result:
you drove twenty minutes to earn 29 cents. Make the tank smaller, the detour
longer, or fill up when the tank's half full, and you're actively paying for
the privilege.

The break-even is easy to state: the detour pays off only when

> price gap per litre × litres filled  >  detour distance × your consumption × price per litre ÷ 100

Two things follow. **Big fills justify detours; top-ups never do.** And the
cheaper your car is to run, the more a detour is worth — a frugal car makes
distant discounts reachable.

## Let the baseline judge your route

The same logic applies to alternate routes to work. Pumpa's calculator has a
**Usual distance** field for exactly this: set your normal route once, and every
trip you price shows the difference against it — in money, not kilometres. A
scenic route that "only" adds 6 km stops being abstract when it reads
`+€0.76` every single workday: that's around €15 a month for one detour choice.

Log the trips you actually drive and the Trips history keeps the running total.
Small route decisions, priced honestly and added up over a month, are usually
worth more than any discount pump within driving distance.
