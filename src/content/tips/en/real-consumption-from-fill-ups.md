Dashboard consumption readouts are optimistic — consistently. Independent tests
regularly find trip computers under-reporting real consumption by 5–10%,
because they estimate fuel flow rather than measure what actually went into the
tank. The pump doesn't flatter you. Your wallet doesn't either.

## The tank-to-tank method

The only equipment you need is the fuel receipt and your odometer:

1. **Fill the tank completely** and note the odometer reading.
2. Drive normally. No special behaviour — the point is *your* real usage.
3. **Fill it completely again.** The litres (or gallons) you just pumped are
   exactly what you burned since the last fill.
4. Divide fuel by the distance between the two odometer readings.

For example: 43.1 litres over 612 km is `43.1 ÷ 612 × 100` = **7.0 L/100km** —
regardless of what the dashboard claimed.

One tank is a decent estimate; three or four consecutive fill-ups make the
average solid, because partial fills and pump cutoff differences even out.
